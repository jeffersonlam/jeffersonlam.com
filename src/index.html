Jefferson Lam

Front End Developer

I build delightful experiences for the web with the users in mind. Previously at Streetspin and IBM.

Work

  • Daytripper

    Playing with Google streetview, I thought to myself, "I wish there was an easier way to see the streetviews down a route." Soon enough, I made an app where you could scroll through Google streetview. After posting it on reddit, it quickly reached 10k unique visitors and 50k API calls in its first week. This was my first app, built during my time at Coding Dojo with plain HTML, CSS, JS, and jQuery. 2014.

  • United Airlines Redesign

    I took on the challenge of creating a mock redesign of United Airline's mobile app. This is a clickable coded prototype of a design concept. 2014.

  • Streetspin

    As part of a 3-person team, we created a game to make learning geography a fun social experience, especially for K-12 education. This was a unique challenge of designing and developing on multiple fronts. We had multiple users – students and teachers – as well as experiences to design and build – game design, the in-game experience, and the "out-game" experience. See it played. 2016 to 2017.

  • Is It Whole30?

    While doing my first Whole30, I often found myself googling additives in grocery store aisles. Frustrated by this tedious and repetitive task, I compiled all the info I could find into a CSV file, converted it into a JSON object (I built a separate tool for that), and wrapped it with a UI built in React to make it immediately searchable. 2018.

  • IBM Community Radio

    Two of my colleagues at IBM thought, "Wouldn't it be cool if we had an internal radio station?" I was one of the initial front-end developers to help prototype this idea, and later worked on a team of 5 to make it real. It quickly took on a life of its own, with IBMers hosting weekly radio shows and enthusiastic listeners tuning in. The platform became recognized by the larger organization and was used to broadcast an interview with Ethan Marcotte. Fast Company describes the project as IBM's unlikely silo buster. 2015.

  • IBM Bluemix

    On Bluemix (now IBM Cloud), I worked closely with designers on a number of projects, including an attempt to re-architect Bluemix, a design experiment around hypothesis driven development, and pushing production code to Bluemix. I also worked with the Orion team to teach and facilitate design thinking, conduct user research, and write code to polish their end-to-end UX. 2015 to 2016

  • IBM Elastic Storage

    I was deployed onto Elastic Storage (now IBM Spectrum Scale) as part of the front lines to revamp the product using design thinking methodologies. I conducted user research to identify holes in the user experience, built a microsite to share said research, and also worked closely with designers to build prototypes for testing out ideas. 2014 to 2015.
